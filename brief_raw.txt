[OVERVIEW]

Al-Shabaab intensified its territorial consolidation strategy in March 2026, executing 40 coordinated attacks against government and AUSSOM forces in Lower Shabelle while dramatically expanding force mobilisation across central regions.¹²³ The group's simultaneous escalation of civilian executions and tactical repositioning signals preparation for sustained offensive operations against Federal control. [Comment: This pattern mirrors pre-offensive behaviour observed in previous territorial expansion phases, though the speed of mobilisation is notably accelerated compared to baseline patterns.]

[THEMATIC ANALYSIS]

**Al-Shabaab operational escalation.** Al-Shabaab conducted 183 events in March, representing a sustained high tempo of operations comparable to recent months.¹⁰ The group's Lower Shabelle campaign targeted government and AUSSOM positions with coordinated attacks in Mubaarak, Daarasalaam and Qoryooley, killing at least 50 personnel in territorial recapture operations on March 2.¹¹¹² [Comment: The concentration of 97 events in Lower Shabelle, compared to more dispersed patterns in baseline months, suggests deliberate territorial focus rather than opportunistic harassment.] Civilian executions increased markedly, with public killings in Saakow (10 people), Jilib (5 people) and Shaw (4 people) following accusations of government collaboration.¹³¹⁴¹⁵

**Federal-SWSPF confrontations.** South-West State Special Police Forces clashed repeatedly with Federal-aligned defectors throughout March, particularly in Bay region around Baidoa, Qansax Dheere and Berdaale.¹⁶¹⁷¹⁸ Multiple SWSPF units defected to Federal alignment, triggering armed confrontations with administration loyalists. Federal forces deployed Harmacad special police to Baraawe and Buur Xakaba, completing territorial control transfers with minimal violence.¹⁹²⁰ [Assumption: Federal strategy prioritised gradual territorial acquisition through defection incentives rather than direct military confrontation. Alternative: SWSPF resistance proved weaker than anticipated, accelerating Federal consolidation beyond planned timelines.]

**ISSP territorial consolidation.** Islamic State Somalia Province established permanent bases in Bari region while conducting systematic attacks against Puntland forces.²¹²² ISSP killed 21 Puntland personnel through coordinated IED strikes on March 12-13, followed by defensive positioning around Dhaadaar and sustained mortar bombardments.²³²⁴²⁵ [Comment: ISSP's base establishment suggests territorial holding intentions rather than purely disruptive operations, representing a strategic shift from previous harassment patterns.]

**Airstrike campaign intensification.** International forces conducted extensive airstrikes across southern regions, killing 231 people in coordinated operations.²⁶²⁷²⁸ The March 9 Dumaaye strike killed over 100 fighters at an al-Shabaab training facility, while sustained targeting in Lower Shabelle supported government territorial operations.²⁹³⁰ Turkish and US forces maintained regular strike patterns against both al-Shabaab and ISSP positions throughout the month.

**Inter-clan violence patterns.** Sanaag region recorded sustained confrontations between Darod-Warsangeli and Isaaq clans over land and mining disputes, culminating in the March 23 Waqdariya clash that killed 8 people.³¹³² Clan violence in Mudug and Galgaduud followed familiar revenge patterns but remained localised rather than escalating to broader factional warfare.

[GEOGRAPHIC FOCUS]

**Lower Shabelle operational theatre.** Lower Shabelle recorded 97 events and 192 fatalities, establishing it as the primary zone of government-al-Shabaab confrontation.³³ The region experienced coordinated al-Shabaab territorial assaults, particularly around Mubaarak and Daarasalaam, where government forces recaptured positions on March 2 before facing sustained counter-attacks throughout the month.³⁴³⁵ According to CHIRPS data, the region faces severe drought at 52% of normal rainfall, potentially affecting population movements and resource competition.³⁶ At 7.2 events per 100,000 population, Lower Shabelle recorded the second-highest per-capita conflict rate after Hiraan, indicating intense security pressures relative to its 1.3 million population. According to harmonised IDP data, the region hosts 95,994 displaced persons, suggesting accumulated displacement pressures from prolonged conflict.³⁷

**Bari strategic significance.** Bari region hosted 44 events despite recording only 14 fatalities, indicating primarily non-lethal strategic developments rather than kinetic operations.³⁸ ISSP's systematic base establishment and Puntland force mobilisation created a sustained confrontation dynamic without major battle casualties until the March 12-13 IED campaign.³⁹⁴⁰ At 4.2 events per 100,000 population, Bari's conflict intensity remained moderate relative to its 1.04 million population, though ISSP territorial consolidation poses long-term governance challenges for Puntland authority. The region's 112,829 IDP population indicates ongoing displacement pressures despite relatively contained violence levels.

**Bay factional battleground.** Bay region recorded 41 events centred on Federal-SWSPF confrontations as the South-West State administration resisted Federal territorial consolidation.⁴¹⁴² Popular demonstrations in Baidoa supported the SWSPF administration while Federal forces established control through selective defection and deployment of special police units.⁴³⁴⁴ At 3.9 events per 100,000 population among Bay's 1.06 million residents, the confrontation represented significant political mobilisation relative to the region's demographic base. According to IPC data, Bay faces Phase 4 Emergency food security with 516,904 people in crisis, creating additional pressures on political stability during the Federal-SWSPF transition.⁴⁵

[TRENDS AND OUTLOOK]

**Territorial consolidation acceleration.** Al-Shabaab's Lower Shabelle operations demonstrate sustained territorial focus compared to the more dispersed patterns visible in baseline months. The concentration of 97 events in a single region, combined with strategic force repositioning documented in strategic developments, suggests preparation for expanded territorial control rather than harassment operations. This pattern is likely to continue in coming months as the group leverages current positioning for broader offensive operations.

**Federal territorial integration.** The South-West State crisis resolved with lower violence than expected, as Federal forces achieved control through selective SWSPF defections rather than sustained military campaigns. This approach is likely to inform Federal strategies in other contested regions, prioritising political accommodation over direct confrontation where possible. However, the Bay region's ongoing tensions suggest incomplete consolidation that may generate future instability.

**ISSP strategic positioning.** Islamic State Somalia Province's transition from harassment operations to territorial base establishment in Bari represents a strategic escalation comparable to al-Shabaab's historical development patterns. The group's systematic attacks on Puntland forces, combined with defensive positioning, indicates preparation for sustained territorial holding operations that are likely to intensify in the coming period.
