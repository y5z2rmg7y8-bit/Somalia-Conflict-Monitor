[OVERVIEW]

Al-Shabaab launched a coordinated assault on Lower Shabelle during March, killing over 100 fighters in airstrikes and ground operations while maintaining tactical pressure across southern regions.¹²³⁴⁵ Simultaneously, the Federal Government's confrontation with South-West State accelerated through military deployments and defections, creating parallel security challenges while ISSP intensified attacks against Puntland forces in Bari. The convergence of al-Shabaab's territorial push, inter-state tensions and ISSP's eastern operations stretched security resources across multiple fronts, complicating counter-insurgency coordination at a critical operational juncture.

[THEMATIC ANALYSIS]

**Al-Shabaab operational intensity.** Al-Shabaab sustained 183 events during March, down from 192 in February, while shifting operational focus towards Lower Shabelle territorial consolidation.¹⁴¹⁵¹⁶ The group established defensive positions in Jamaame district¹⁷ and concentrated forces in Qoryooley, prompting intensive counter-operations that killed over 100 fighters in coordinated airstrikes and ground assaults.¹⁸¹⁹²⁰²¹ [Comment: The Lower Shabelle concentration suggests al-Shabaab is prioritising territorial control over dispersed harassment operations, potentially indicating resource constraints or strategic realignment towards defensible strongholds.]

**Airstrikes and targeted killings.** International forces and SNA conducted 70 remote violence events, killing 231 people—the second-highest monthly total in the baseline period.²² US forces alone conducted 10 airstrikes, primarily targeting ISSP positions in Bari mountains.²³²⁴²⁵ Turkish forces maintained pressure on al-Shabaab gatherings in agricultural areas.²⁶ [Comment: The surge in airstrike fatalities reflects both target availability from al-Shabaab force concentration and improved intelligence coordination, though the sustainability of this tempo depends on continued target identification.]

**Federal-Southwest confrontation.** Federal forces deployed special police units to Baraawe and Buur Hakaba²⁷²⁸ while SWSPF units defected to Federal alignment across Bay region. SWSPF forces abandoned Baraawe entirely, relocating to Kismayo by boat.²⁹ Clashes between Federal-aligned and administration-loyal SWSPF occurred in 13 separate incidents, indicating systematic rather than isolated resistance. [Assumption: Federal operations prioritise territorial control through defection incentives rather than direct military confrontation. Alternative: SWSPF collapses reflect unpaid salaries and institutional weakness rather than effective Federal pressure.]

**ISSP expansion in Bari.** ISSP maintained tactical momentum with 29 events, establishing new bases near Dhaadaar³⁰ and conducting systematic mortar attacks against Puntland positions.³¹³² The group killed 14 Puntland Dervish Force personnel through IED attacks and ambushes³³³⁴, demonstrating improved explosive capabilities and tactical coordination. Strategic developments show ISSP forces regrouping in multiple mountain locations simultaneously³⁵³⁶, suggesting expanded territorial control beyond previous operational zones.

**Inter-clan violence patterns.** Sanaag recorded 23 events with clan militia clashes concentrated around Waqdariya and Shidan villages, killing 8 in the month's lethal incident.³⁷ Land disputes drove most confrontations, with Darod-Warsangeli and Isaaq-Habar Jeclo factions maintaining territorial competition despite traditional mediation attempts. Galdogob witnessed renewed Majeerteen sub-clan fighting with 3 killed³⁸, indicating persistent resource-based tensions in pastoral areas.

[GEOGRAPHIC FOCUS]

**Lower Shabelle.** Al-Shabaab's territorial push in Qoryooley and Afgooye districts prompted the month's substantial fighting, with 192 fatalities across 97 events. The group concentrated forces around Mubaarak village³⁹, leading to intensive AUSSOM and SNA counter-operations that reclaimed territory at significant cost.⁴⁰⁴¹ According to IPC data, Lower Shabelle faces Crisis conditions (Phase 3+) affecting 448,800 people, while CHIRPS indicates severe drought at 52% of normal rainfall—the worst in the country. At 7.2 events per 100,000 population, Lower Shabelle ranks second nationally for conflict intensity, reflecting both al-Shabaab pressure and humanitarian vulnerabilities. According to harmonised IDP data, the region hosts 95,994 displaced persons, suggesting population movements from rural areas under al-Shabaab control towards government-held towns.

**Bari.** ISSP operations across 44 events killed 14 Puntland forces while establishing mountain strongholds through 15 strategic developments. The group's systematic targeting of Dervish Force convoys demonstrates tactical evolution beyond opportunistic attacks.⁴²⁴³ At 4.2 events per 100,000 population, Bari's conflict intensity remains moderate despite ISSP expansion. According to IPC data, Phase 4 Emergency conditions affect 430,730 people in crisis or worse, with 112,458 in emergency phase. CHIRPS shows below-average rainfall at 92% of normal, creating pastoral stress without reaching drought thresholds. The presence of 112,829 IDPs according to harmonised data indicates displacement from ISSP-affected areas towards urban centres like Bosaso.

**Middle Shabelle.** The region sustained 42 events with 54 fatalities, maintaining February's operational tempo despite predictions of territorial consolidation escalation. Al-Shabaab concentrated over 200 fighters in Miiro village⁴⁴ while government forces conducted operations around Balcad and Xawaadleey, killing 24 fighters in coordinated ground and air operations.⁴⁵⁴⁶⁴⁷ According to IPC data, 267,958 people face Crisis or worse conditions, with drought at 63% of normal rainfall according to CHIRPS. At 4.9 events per 100,000 population, Middle Shabelle ranks fourth nationally for conflict intensity, while hosting 81,368 IDPs in displacement sites near Jowhar and Balcad.

[TRENDS AND OUTLOOK]

**Al-Shabaab territorial strategy.** Comparison with baseline months shows al-Shabaab maintaining 183 events in March 2026 versus 167 events in March 2024, indicating sustained operational capacity despite airstrikes. The group's Lower Shabelle concentration mirrors seasonal patterns from 2024-2025, when territorial pushes preceded rainy season logistics preparations. Al-Shabaab's defensive positions in Jamaame and force regrouping in agricultural areas suggest preparation for sustained confrontation rather than tactical withdrawal. [Comment: Historical patterns suggest al-Shabaab territorial consolidation attempts typically intensify during March-April, preceding Gu season mobility constraints.]

**Airstrike effectiveness and limitations.** March's 70 remote violence events with 231 fatalities represents a 40% increase in lethality compared to February's 96 events with 222 fatalities, indicating improved targeting precision. However, al-Shabaab's continued operational capacity across multiple regions suggests tactical adaptation rather than strategic degradation. The group's force regrouping patterns show decentralisation responses to air pressure while maintaining coordinated operations. Remote violence incidents are likely to maintain intensity through April as force concentrations provide target opportunities.

**Federal-Southwest dynamics.** SWSPF defections accelerated through March with 13 confrontation events compared to baseline averages of 3-5 monthly Federal-state clashes. The complete SWSPF withdrawal from Baraawe and Federal deployment to Buur Hakaba indicate systematic territorial transfer rather than negotiated resolution. Federal consolidation is likely to continue through Bay region as remaining SWSPF units face isolation and salary pressures. Opposition group formation around Baidoa⁴⁸ suggests resistance will persist despite territorial losses.

**ISSP tactical evolution.** ISSP's 29 March events represent the group's highest monthly total since July 2025, with systematic targeting replacing opportunistic attacks. The establishment of multiple mountain bases and coordinated mortar attacks indicate territorial ambitions beyond previous hit-and-run tactics. ISSP operations are likely to intensify through April as spring conditions facilitate mountain mobility, while Puntland forces face resource constraints across expanded operational zones.
