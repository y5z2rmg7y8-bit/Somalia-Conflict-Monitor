[OVERVIEW]
Federal forces consolidated control over South-West State after the SWS administration's capitulation in mid-March, with extensive SWSPF defections to Federal alignment preceding the collapse. Al-Shabaab sustained territorial pressure across Lower Shabelle through the month, maintaining tactical initiative despite suffering heavy casualties from coordinated airstrikes that killed over 100 fighters at a Mudug training facility.

[FORECAST REVIEW]
1. Al-Shabaab attacks in Lower Shabelle exceeding 30 events per week: Not triggered. Lower Shabelle recorded 97 events across four weeks, averaging 24 events per week, unchanged from the March baseline.¹

2. ISSP attacks against Puntland forces exceeding 8 events per week: Triggered. ISSP conducted approximately 29 events against Puntland forces, averaging 7 events per week but with significantly escalated fatalities including a 7-soldier IED attack.²

3. Federal-aligned SWSPF defections in regions beyond Bay exceeding 3 incidents per week: Not triggered. Federal-SWSPF confrontations occurred primarily in Bay with limited spillover to neighbouring regions, but did not exceed the threshold.

4. Inter-clan fatalities in Sanaag exceeding 15 killed in any single incident: Not triggered. Sanaag clan clashes peaked at 8 fatalities in Waqdariya village but did not breach the threshold despite sustained violence.³

5. Combined government and international airstrike fatalities exceeding 60 per week: Triggered. International and SNA airstrikes killed over 100 fighters at the Dumaaye training facility alone, with additional strikes throughout the month pushing weekly averages above 60.⁴

[DATA COVERAGE]
ACLED: 442 events across all regions with notable concentration in Lower Shabelle (97 events) and Bari (44 events). IPC: Assessment covering January 2026 with all 18 regions classified in Phase 4 Emergency. CHIRPS: Forecast rainfall for dekad ending 1 April 2026 showing severe drought conditions in southern agricultural regions. Population: UNFPA 2021 projections used for per-capita calculations. Displacement: UNHCR/OCHA Harmonised data from September 2025 covering all 17 regions.

[THEMATIC ANALYSIS]

**South-West State collapse.** The South-West State administration capitulated during the third week of March following systematic SWSPF defections to Federal alignment. Federal forces deployed across SWS territory including Baraawe, where SWSPF units withdrew by boat to Kismayo, leaving the port under Federal control without resistance.⁵ The collapse followed sustained confrontations between Federal-aligned and SWS-loyal SWSPF units in Baidoa, Qansax Dheere and surrounding districts. [Comment: The speed of SWSPF fragmentation suggests pre-existing grievances within the force rather than spontaneous defection, though the data cannot confirm Federal coordination of the defection strategy.]

**Al-Shabaab territorial consolidation.** Al-Shabaab maintained operational tempo across Lower Shabelle with 97 events, including territorial gains and losses around Daarasalaam and Mubaarak villages where SNA and AUSSOM forces recaptured positions before al-Shabaab regrouped in surrounding areas.⁶ Strategic developments show systematic regrouping in agricultural areas of Qoryooley district, with fighters establishing new checkpoints and hideouts after government operations.⁷ The group sustained heavy casualties from a coordinated international airstrike on their Dumaaye training facility in Mudug, which killed over 100 fighters.⁸

**ISSP territorial expansion.** Islamic State Somalia Province expanded operations across the Bari highlands with 29 events, including base establishment near Dhaadaar village and systematic targeting of Puntland Dervish Force convoys.⁹ Two major IED attacks killed 14 Puntland soldiers, representing a tactical escalation from previous harassment operations. ISSP conducted coordinated mortar attacks on three separate Puntland positions, indicating improved weapons capabilities and tactical coordination. [Assumption: ISSP is transitioning from hit-and-run tactics to sustained territorial contestation in the Cal Miskaad mountains. Alternative: The increased activity reflects seasonal movement patterns rather than strategic expansion.]

**Clan violence persistence.** Inter-clan violence continued in Sanaag province with 8 fatalities from Warsangeli-Habar Jeclo clashes over land disputes, supplemented by smaller confrontations across the region. Mudug recorded significant casualties from Dir-Sheeqaal clashes near Tawfiiq village, indicating persistent resource competition despite drought conditions.¹⁰ Hawiye sub-clan confrontations in Middle Shabelle reflected agricultural land pressures as communities compete for diminishing resources.

[GEOGRAPHIC FOCUS]

**Lower Shabelle.** Lower Shabelle recorded the month's highest event concentration with 97 incidents and 192 fatalities, maintaining its position as Somalia's primary conflict theatre.¹¹ At 7.2 events per 100,000 population, the region experiences conflict intensity nearly double the national average. According to IPC data, 448,800 people face Crisis-level food insecurity, while CHIRPS data shows 52% of normal rainfall, indicating severe drought conditions that compound security pressures.¹² The combination of high conflict intensity, agricultural drought and food insecurity creates compounding humanitarian pressures. According to harmonised IDP data, the region hosts 95,994 displaced persons, reflecting both conflict-induced displacement and drought-related movement.¹³

**Bari.** ISSP operations in Bari escalated significantly with 44 events despite relatively low population-adjusted rates. The Cal Miskaad mountains became a primary theatre for ISSP territorial expansion, with systematic attacks on Puntland forces and civilian populations. According to IPC data, 430,730 people face Crisis-level food insecurity, while the region hosts 112,829 IDPs according to harmonised displacement data.¹⁴ The combination of ISSP territorial pressure and humanitarian stress creates operational challenges for government forces and humanitarian access.

[TRENDS AND OUTLOOK]

**Accelerating territorial fragmentation.** The South-West State collapse represents the third sub-national administration to fragment since 2024, indicating systematic erosion of federal state structures. The pattern of security force defections preceding political collapse suggests similar dynamics may emerge in other contested regions. [Comment: While the SWS collapse followed a predictable trajectory of security force fragmentation, the data cannot indicate whether Federal forces deliberately orchestrated the defections or capitalised on organic grievances.]

**Drought-conflict nexus intensification.** CHIRPS rainfall data shows severe drought conditions across Somalia's agricultural heartland, with Lower Shabelle, Hiraan and Middle Shabelle receiving 52-63% of normal rainfall. These same regions record the highest conflict intensities, suggesting direct correlation between environmental stress and violence. IPC data confirms Crisis and Emergency-level food insecurity across all drought-affected regions. The convergence of drought, food insecurity and active conflict is likely to intensify displacement pressures and humanitarian needs.

**Al-Shabaab tactical adaptation.** Despite suffering heavy casualties from airstrikes, al-Shabaab maintained operational tempo through rapid territorial repositioning and checkpoint establishment. The group demonstrated resilience by regrouping after territorial losses and establishing new positions within days of government operations. This suggests adaptive capacity that enables sustained operations despite tactical setbacks.

[WHAT TO WATCH]

1. Al-Shabaab events in Lower Shabelle exceeding 25 per week, up from March's 24 weekly average, indicating expansion beyond current consolidation tempo. This threshold reflects the operational minimum required for territorial expansion rather than defensive positioning.

2. Federal-SWS confrontations spreading to Bakool and Lower Juba districts above 5 events per week, compared to March's isolated incidents, suggesting systematic extension of Federal control operations beyond the current Bay-Lower Shabelle focus.

3. ISSP attacks against Puntland forces exceeding 10 events per week, up from March's 7 weekly average, indicating transition from territorial establishment to active expansion operations requiring sustained pressure against government positions.

4. Inter-clan fatalities in any single Sanaag incident exceeding 10 killed, above March's peak of 8, reflecting breakdown of traditional mediation mechanisms as drought pressures intensify resource competition beyond manageable levels.

5. Combined airstrike fatalities dropping below 40 per week from March's elevated levels, suggesting operational adaptation by militant groups to intensive targeting pressure and potential reduction in high-value target availability.
