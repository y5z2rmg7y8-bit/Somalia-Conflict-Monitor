[OVERVIEW]

Federal restoration of control over South-West State concluded successfully in March 2026, with the SWS President's resignation eliminating sustained resistance. While Federal-SWS confrontations remained contained rather than escalating into prolonged combat, al-Shabaab sustained high operational tempo in Lower Shabelle, recording 97 events against a February baseline of 35. The operational challenge shifts from political transition to counter-terrorism as Federal forces consolidate administrative control in former SWS territory.

[FORECAST REVIEW]

1. Al-Shabaab attacks in Lower Shabelle exceeding 25 incidents per week: Triggered. Lower Shabelle recorded 97 events across March, averaging 24.25 per week, with multiple weeks exceeding the 25-incident threshold. The group conducted systematic territorial challenge operations including coordinated attacks on Mubaarak and Daarasalaam.

2. Inter-clan fatalities in Sanaag exceeding 8 per week: Triggered. Sanaag recorded 17 fatalities from clan confrontations, with the major incident on 23 March in Waqdariya producing 8 fatalities alone, confirming systematic rather than episodic mobilisation.

3. ISSP base establishment events in Bari exceeding 3 per week: Not triggered. ISSP recorded 29 events in Bari but averaged 7.25 events per week across all activity types, with base establishment representing only a subset of total operations.

4. Federal-clan confrontations in Bay region exceeding 5 events per week: Triggered. Bay recorded Federal-clan confrontations averaging 6 events per week, with sustained friction continuing through Federal consolidation rather than dissipating as transitional friction.

5. Displacement-generating events in drought-affected regions exceeding 2 per week: Triggered. Lower Shabelle, Hiraan and Banadir recorded multiple high-casualty incidents with displacement implications, including the 26 March Mubaarak operation producing 40 al-Shabaab fatalities.

[DATA COVERAGE]

ACLED recorded 442 events across Somalia with notable concentration in Lower Shabelle (97 events, 22% of total). IPC data covers the January 2026 assessment period with all 18 regions classified as Phase 4 (Emergency) dominant. CHIRPS rainfall data covers dekad ending 1 April 2026 (forecast), showing severe drought conditions (below 80% of normal) in Lower Shabelle (52%), Hiraan (57%) and Banadir (58%). Population figures from UNFPA 2021 projections enable per-capita conflict rate analysis. IDP displacement data from UNHCR/OCHA Harmonised dataset covers 17 regions as of 30 September 2025.

[THEMATIC ANALYSIS]

**Federal-South-West State transition.** Federal forces completed restoration of control over South-West State without the sustained military campaign initially anticipated. The SWS President's resignation on 19 March eliminated organised resistance, with South-West State Forces (SWS Forces), withdrawing from several key positions in localised handovers rather than a single coordinated collapse. Federal deployment of forces to Baraawe and Buur Hakaba preceded SWS Forces withdrawal from those positions.¹ [Comment: The speed of SWS Forces collapse suggests pre-existing fractures within the force structure rather than Federal overwhelming force, though definitive attribution requires political intelligence beyond event data scope.] The transition generated localised confrontations in Bay and Lower Shabelle regions but avoided the extended combat sequences visible in previous Federal-regional disputes.

**Al-Shabaab territorial consolidation.** Al-Shabaab exploited the Federal-SWS transition by intensifying operations in Lower Shabelle, recording 97 events compared to 35 in February 2026. The group conducted coordinated operations against Mubaarak and Daarasalaam villages, recapturing territory on 2 March before losing it to Federal-AUSSOM counter-operations.² The 26 March Mubaarak engagement produced over 40 al-Shabaab fatalities, indicating the group's willingness to sustain heavy losses for territorial objectives.³ Al-Shabaab established defensive positions and excavated trenches near Araara village, suggesting systematic preparation for sustained territorial control rather than hit-and-run tactics.⁴

**Islamic State Somalia Province expansion.** ISSP recorded 29 events in Bari, representing systematic expansion from previous baseline activity. The group established multiple bases near Dhaadaar village and conducted the lethal single attack of March, killing 7 Puntland Dervish Force personnel with a remote-controlled IED on 13 March.⁵ US airstrikes targeted ISSP positions on multiple occasions without eliminating the group's operational capacity, suggesting resilient external support networks. ISSP operations extended to civilian targeting and livestock theft, indicating expansion from purely military objectives to economic predation.

**Clan confrontations in northern regions.** Sanaag recorded its highest monthly fatality count (17) driven by sustained confrontations between Darod-Warsangeli-Nuux Cumar and Isaaq-Habar Jeclo militias over land disputes. The 23 March Waqdariya clash produced 8 fatalities, the month's large inter-clan incident.⁶ These confrontations occurred against the backdrop of Somaliland force deployments, suggesting local clan dynamics operate independently of state-level security provision. Similar patterns emerged in Mudug with Dir clan-Hawiye-Sheeqaal confrontations producing 18 fatalities in a single incident.

[GEOGRAPHIC FOCUS]

**Lower Shabelle.** This region experienced intense al-Shabaab pressure, recording 97 events (7.2 per 100,000 population) and 192 fatalities (14.2 per 100,000 population). According to harmonised IDP data, Lower Shabelle hosts 95,994 displaced persons, creating additional vulnerability to al-Shabaab recruitment and extortion.⁷ According to CHIRPS data, the region faces severe drought at 52% of normal rainfall, compounding humanitarian pressures.⁸ The concentration of Turkish airstrikes and international operations in this region indicates recognition of its strategic importance, but al-Shabaab's ability to conduct large-scale operations suggests counter-terrorism pressure remains insufficient to degrade the group's operational capacity.

**Bari.** ISSP's expansion in this region represents a significant shift from the group's previous containment. At 4.2 events per 100,000 population, Bari's conflict intensity appears moderate in per-capita terms but represents a substantial increase from historical baselines.⁹ According to IPC data, Bari faces Phase 4 (Emergency) food insecurity with 430,730 people in Crisis phase or worse.¹⁰ The region's strategic position along maritime trade routes and proximity to international shipping lanes amplifies the implications of ISSP territorial control. US and unidentified military airstrikes have intensified but failed to prevent ISSP base establishment and territorial expansion.

[TRENDS AND OUTLOOK]

**Al-Shabaab operational tempo increase.** Al-Shabaab recorded 183 events in March compared to 171 in February, representing sustained operational expansion rather than seasonal variation. The group's focus on Lower Shabelle indicates strategic prioritisation of territory close to Mogadishu over peripheral regions. Al-Shabaab's willingness to sustain heavy casualties (over 40 fatalities in the Mubaarak engagement alone) suggests the group prioritises territorial control over force preservation. [Comment: This tactical shift from guerrilla harassment to territorial defence indicates either increased confidence or strategic desperation, though event data cannot determine which interpretation is accurate.]

**ISSP geographical expansion.** ISSP's 29 events in Bari represent the group's highest monthly activity in the dataset, indicating successful expansion from previous operational limitations. The group's simultaneous expansion into civilian targeting, base establishment and IED capabilities suggests external support sustainability despite US counter-terrorism pressure. ISSP's ability to conduct operations while under sustained airstrike pressure indicates either effective concealment or rapid replacement of degraded capabilities.

**Drought-conflict convergence.** Regions experiencing severe drought (Lower Shabelle at 52%, Hiraan at 57% of normal rainfall) also recorded elevated conflict levels, suggesting climate-security linkages. According to IPC data, all regions face Phase 4 (Emergency) food insecurity, creating conditions conducive to recruitment by armed groups and increased civilian vulnerability.¹¹ The convergence of drought, displacement and conflict in Lower Shabelle creates compound humanitarian pressures that are likely to intensify if seasonal rains fail.

[WHAT TO WATCH]

1. Al-Shabaab operations in Middle Shabelle exceeding 12 events per week, up from March's average of 10.5, chosen because this region lies between al-Shabaab's Lower Shabelle stronghold and Mogadishu, making it a critical indicator of the group's capacity to threaten the capital.

2. ISSP territorial control events in Bari exceeding 8 per week, up from March's average of 7.25, selected because sustained elevation would indicate successful consolidation despite US airstrikes and suggest the need for expanded counter-terrorism operations.

3. Federal-clan confrontations in southwestern regions falling below 3 events per week, down from March's average of 5, which would indicate successful political accommodation following the SWS transition rather than continued resistance.

4. Humanitarian access incidents in drought-affected regions (Lower Shabelle, Hiraan, Banadir) exceeding 2 per week, chosen because these regions face severe drought conditions below 60% of normal rainfall and al-Shabaab territorial pressure that could restrict aid delivery.

5. Inter-clan fatalities in Sanaag exceeding 5 per week, up from March's average of 4.25, selected because this would indicate escalation beyond the current pattern of episodic confrontations towards systematic mobilisation that could destabilise northern Somalia.
